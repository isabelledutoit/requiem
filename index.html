The Series

In Requiem, Isabelle du Toit turns the hyperrealist gaze toward objects that have outlasted their purpose — driftwood bearing wounds, bone-pale forms concealing interior worlds, surfaces that bleed without warning. Each painting is an act of witness. A mourning that refuses sentiment.
